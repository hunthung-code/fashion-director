# ECHO TAIPEI — Target Audience

Three personas. Real enough to design for, fictional enough to be safe to share.

---

## Persona 1 — 柏諺 (Bo-Yan), 24

**Tagline:** He was there before it had a name.

**Life:** Junior sound engineer at an independent label in Da'an. Goes to 2–3 shows a week. Doesn't post much. When he does, it's a photo of a venue before doors open, no caption.

**Relationship with fashion:**
- Functional first. He needs layers he can move in, carry a bag, sit on a floor.
- Quality matters because he keeps things 5+ years. Fast fashion feels dishonest.
- Hates being seen trying. His entire aesthetic is "I wasn't thinking about it."
- Buys maybe 6 pieces a year. Each one considered.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Dress in a way that reads as belonging without signaling effort
- Find clothes that still look right at 3am and at a client meeting at 11am
- Own things that don't date — he's exhausted by trend cycles

**Sensitivity:**
- Instantly rejects anything with visible branding, influencer association, or hype drops
- Respects craftsmanship claims only if supported by material evidence (not marketing copy)

---

## Persona 2 — 子晴 (Zi-Ching), 27

**Tagline:** She built the scene; the scene doesn't know her name.

**Life:** Co-runs a small creative agency doing brand work for local music and food venues. Dresses for herself, not the feed — but the feed notices anyway.

**Relationship with fashion:**
- Deliberately mixes high and very low. NT$12,000 outerwear over NT$150 market tee.
- Doesn't follow fashion media. Discovers through physical experience: walking around, markets, friends.
- Has strong opinions about what Taipei "should" look like vs. what it currently looks like visually.
- Would never describe herself as "into fashion." Is very much into fashion.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Express a specific cultural perspective, not just an aesthetic
- Find pieces that work in multiple contexts — client pitch, Luzhou night market, Revolver afterparty
- Support brands that feel locally rooted (not just locally inspired)

**Sensitivity:**
- Immediate distrust of anything that feels like it's performing localness for export
- Knows immediately when a brand's Taipei references are surface-level
- Will recommend ECHO to three people or zero — no in-between

---

## Persona 3 — 家豪 (Jia-Hao), 22

**Tagline:** He doesn't know the reference yet, but he will.

**Life:** Third-year design student. Part-time at a coffee shop in Zhongshan. Just discovering that he has taste, and it's somewhat overwhelming.

**Relationship with fashion:**
- Still in the phase of collecting references — bookmarking things he can't fully explain yet.
- Budget-constrained. One or two bigger purchases per year, the rest second-hand or basics.
- Aspirationally aware of Bo-Yan types but not yet confident enough to dress fully that way.
- Will grow into a core customer in 2–3 years.

**Jobs-to-be-done:**
- Find his visual identity without making obvious mistakes
- Access quality without full-price commitment (second-hand ECHO pieces already circulate)
- Understand what his aesthetic actually is by process of elimination

**Sensitivity:**
- Extremely online — will notice if a brand behaves differently across platforms
- Can smell inauthenticity but sometimes can't articulate why something feels wrong
- The first person in his friend group to discover new things; the last to talk about it

---

## Audience notes for skills

**For `creative-direction`:** Bo-Yan is the primary. Design for him and Zi-Ching will respect it. Jia-Hao will aspire to it.

**For `seasonal-curation`:** Bo-Yan and Zi-Ching determine what ECHO adopts. Jia-Hao is the early signal-catcher (what's coming next season in his feeds).

**For `brand-audit`:** If the brand's communication sounds like it's designed to impress Jia-Hao at the expense of losing Bo-Yan and Zi-Ching, that's a failure state.

**For `market-strategy`:** All three personas are price-sensitive but quality-respecting. NT$2,000–3,500 is the sweet spot for repeat purchase. Above NT$8,000, the story needs to be exceptional.

**For `lookbook-generator`:** No models in the traditional sense. Real environments, real people from the scene. If using placeholders, the description should specify environment over model type.
