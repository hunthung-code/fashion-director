# ECHO TAIPEI — Brand Guidelines

## Brand essence

**One line:** Taipei's underground, made wearable.

**Elevator pitch:** ECHO TAIPEI makes quality basics and limited drops for the people who stay after the show ends — the builders, the DJs, the skaters, the night market regulars. Not loud. Not for everyone. Exactly right for the ones who get it.

---

## Brand DNA — 3 core words

**Understated. Grounded. Deliberate.**

- **Understated** — we don't explain ourselves. The work speaks. No giant logos, no trend-chasing, no influencer flood.
- **Grounded** — we come from Taipei, specifically. Da'an nights, Zhongshan coffee-shop afternoons, Xinyi after midnight. Not "Asia" as a vague aesthetic — actual Taipei.
- **Deliberate** — small drops, done well. We'd rather make 200 units of something right than 2,000 units of something fine.

## Visual identity

### Color system

| Token | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| `--c-primary` | Tungsten | `#2C2C2E` | Primary backgrounds, dominant surfaces |
| `--c-secondary` | Raw Cream | `#F5F0E8` | Text on dark, light-mode surface |
| `--c-accent` | Oxide Red | `#C94C3A` | CTA, highlight, seasonal accent |
| `--c-mid` | Concrete | `#8A8A8E` | Secondary text, borders, captions |
| `--c-warm` | Aged Paper | `#D4C9B0` | Seasonal texture, editorial backgrounds |

**Dark base first.** Tungsten is default. Raw Cream on Tungsten is the hero combination.

### Typography

| Role | Style | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Display | Extended grotesque (e.g., Neue Haas Grotesk Extended, or system fallback: Arial Black) | Industrial, confident, no ornament |
| Body | Regular weight grotesque | Same family, lighter — readable, not loud |
| Caption / micro | All-caps, tracked wide (letter-spacing: 0.15em) | Editorial signal |

**No serif.** No script. Nothing that reads decorative.

### Photography / imagery direction

- **Real environments:** Taipei streets, parking garages, wet markets at 6am, rooftops.
- **No studio white backgrounds.** We don't do traditional product photography.
- **People:** candid-adjacent. Not posed-editorial. Real faces, not model-book faces.
- **Color treatment:** slightly desaturated, pushed toward cool-mid tones. Not washed out — just not oversaturated.
- **Ratio:** 4:5 for social, 3:4 for editorial spreads, 1:1 for product.

### Motion

- Slow. Nothing bounces. Nothing springs.
- Transitions: ease-out, 400–600ms. Not snappy — considered.
- No particle effects, no glitch, no hype-culture motion clichés.

## Verbal identity / tone of voice

### Personality

Quiet confidence. We speak like someone who doesn't need to raise their voice.

### What we sound like

- Short sentences.
- First person rarely. Third person or imperative.
- No superlatives. "Best" / "perfect" / "amazing" → cut.
- Numbers over adjectives. "200 units" not "limited run."
- City-specific when it matters. "Da'an" not "Taipei" not "Taiwan" not "Asia."

### Tone examples

| ✅ ECHO | ❌ Not ECHO |
|---|---|
| "200 pieces. Tungsten fleece. Drop Friday." | "Our amazing new limited-edition fleece is finally here!" |
| "Made for Taipei nights." | "Inspired by Asian street culture." |
| "No restock." | "Hurry — selling fast!" |
| "Wear it until it looks like yours." | "Premium quality guaranteed!" |

### Social bio formula

`[City signal]. [Category signal]. [Drop cadence].`

Example: `台北地下 / Taipei underground. Basics and limited drops. No restock.`

## What ECHO TAIPEI rejects (the rejection list)

- **Loud branding:** chest-logo tees, all-over prints, obvious streetwear signifiers.
- **Pan-Asian vagueness:** "Asian aesthetic" / "Eastern vibes" / cherry blossoms.
- **Hype mechanics:** countdown timers, FOMO language, artificial scarcity theater.
- **Fast trend adoption:** if a macro trend peaks, we wait until it becomes cultural DNA before touching it.
- **Influencer aesthetics:** over-produced, over-lit, everyone looks the same.
- **Gen Z clichés:** Y2K revival, corporate-core irony, oversaturated color for its own sake.
